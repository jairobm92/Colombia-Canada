Hiring through the FTA between Colombia and Canada

Position: Automotive Service Mechanics Foreman/Woman

1. Introduction to the Colombia-Canada FTA

  • The Free Trade Agreement (FTA) between Colombia and Canada came into effect on August 15, 2011.
  • This agreement not only facilitates trade in goods and services but also promotes labor mobility between both countries.
  • It establishes a legal framework for the temporary entry of business persons, professionals, and specialized technicians.

2. Benefits of the FTA for Labor Mobility

  • Simplifies immigration procedures and work permits for qualified Colombians.
  • Does not require a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) for certain professional categories.
  • Allows Canadian companies to hire Colombian talent through more streamlined procedures.
  • Work permits under the FTA can be extended for up to one year and are renewable.
  • Possibility to subsequently apply for permanent residency through provincial programs.

3. Specific Category: Automotive Service Mechanics Foreman/Woman

National Occupational Classification (NOC):

  • NOC Code: 72024 (Automotive Service Mechanics Foreman/Woman)
  • Skill Level: B (Technician/Technologist)
  • This classification is specifically included in the professional annex of the Colombia-Canada FTA.

Specific Requirements for this Position:

  • Diploma or certificate in automotive mechanics or related field
  • Minimum 2 years of experience in the automotive sector.
  • Specialized technical knowledge in diagnosis, repair, and maintenance of vehicles

Specific Documentation Required:

  • Apostilled academic degree or certificate
  • Letters of recommendation and employment certificates demonstrating supervisory experience
  • Formal job offer from the Canadian company specifying the Foreman/Woman position
  • Documentation demonstrating compliance with provincial licensing requirements (if applicable)

5. Hiring Process through the FTA

  • Formal job offer from the Canadian company
  • Preparation of specific documentation for TN visa (FTA Professional)
  • Application to the Canadian consulate in Colombia or at the port of entry to Canada
  • Estimated processing times (generally faster than other programs)
  • Support from a specialized consultant throughout the process

6. Benefits for the Canadian Company

  • Faster hiring process compared to other immigration pathways
  • Cost savings by avoiding the LMIA process
  • Hiring personnel with specific experience in the automotive sector
  • Potential knowledge transfer and techniques from the Colombian market
  • Possibility of long-term retention through permanent residency sponsorship
